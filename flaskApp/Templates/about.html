About Heart Disease

Heart disease (heart disease) is a group of diseases related to cardiovascular diseases, manifested by a violation of the normal functioning of the heart. May be caused by damage to the epicardium, pericardium, myocardium, endocardium, valvular apparatus of the heart, heart vessels.

According to the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute in Framingham (USA), the most important factors in the development of cardiovascular disease in humans are obesity, sedentary lifestyle and smoking.

17.9M Deaths per year globally
#1 Cause of death worldwide
80% Preventable cases

Key Risk Factors

🍔

Obesity

Excess weight puts extra strain on the heart and increases risk of heart disease.

🛋️

Sedentary Lifestyle

Lack of physical activity weakens the heart muscle and contributes to weight gain.

🚬

Smoking

Tobacco use damages blood vessels and significantly increases heart disease risk.

🍷

Alcohol

Excessive alcohol consumption can lead to high blood pressure and heart failure.

🩺

Diabetes

High blood sugar levels damage blood vessels and increase cardiovascular risk.

😰

Stress

Chronic stress can contribute to high blood pressure and unhealthy coping behaviors.
